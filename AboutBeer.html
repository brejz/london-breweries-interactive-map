About Beer

 

What is Beer ?

Beer is one of the oldest and most largely consumed alcoholic beverage in the world. Beer is made with malted cereal grains (which could include barley, wheat, rye, corn or rice), hops and water that is fermented by adding yeast. Yeast eats sugar, divides (then multiplies), and produces carbon dioxide and ethanol (alcohol).

Ale -
Brewed with top fermenting yeast at cellar temperature, ales are fuller-bodied, with nuances of fruit or spice and a pleasantly hoppy finish.
Ales include Bitters, Milds, Abbey Ales, Pale Ales, Nut Browns, etc
Lager -
A lager, which can range from sweet to bitter and pale to black is often used to describe bottom-fermented brews of Dutch, German, and Czech styles.
Most lager, however, are a pale to medium colour, have high carbonation, and a medium to high hop flavour.
Malt -
Generally dark and sweeter in flavour, malts contain hints of caramel, toffee, and nuts. They can be light to full bodied.
A speciality beer is a high quality beer with a distinctive taste profile which appeals to senses, demonstrates integrity, encourages experimentation, and stimulates debates.
This could also refer to any limited time beers that there are on offer.
Stout & Porter -.
Stout, features a rich, creamy head and is flavoured and coloured by barley.
Porter is a dark, almost black, fruity-dry, top fermenting style
